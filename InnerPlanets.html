Mercury

Mercury has hardly any atmosphere, orbits the sun at around 50km/sec and its surface temperature can excede 400 degrees celsius... hot enough for ya?

Despite being the second smallest planet in the Solar System, there is still plenty to see and do - take for example the Caloris Basin. It is one of the largest features on Mercury, and is about 1,300 km in diameter and was caused by an asteroid impact on the planet's surface early in the solar system's history. One could also explore the mysterious surface features that are considered by many to be caused by ancient alien life-forms (though others feel that they were more likely caused by the massive asteroid impact on the opposite side of the planet).

Venus
Referred to by many as Earth's sister planet, Venus is named after the Roman goddess of love - and it is easy to see why. Venus is covered by thick clouds which both obscure the true surface of the planet, and reflect light back - making it one of the brightest planets visible in the sky. If one gets past the shining facade, Venus is unstable and volcanic, with crushing pressures, surface temperatures hot enough to boil lead and the air is toxic... but hey, that's love for you!
